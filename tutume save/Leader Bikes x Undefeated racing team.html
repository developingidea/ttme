About

We teamed up with Undefeated for you 2016 race team to represent the LA bicycle race culture. Selecting the strongest rider based out of the LA area, the team will be racing all over the world for the 2016 race season, from LA to NYC to Europe. Starting off the race season, and launching the 2016 Undefeated x Leader Team and new gear, RHC Brooklyn( April 30th) will be the first opportunity to check out this years team, then in LA for WPH Long Beach May7th. Our concept bikes will be available at the Undefeated and Leader Bikes in USA and JAPAN on May 21st.

Riders

All of our team riders are based out of the greater LA area. They not only compete in LA races, but nation wide Leaders in and out of the race culture, representing LA and Leader Bikes.

Alonso Tal

a African Descent-American & progressive fixed gear and road cyclist based out of the Greater Los Angeles area. Introduced to track bikes in 2005, Alonso quickly earned a reputation as being a skilled brakeless riders in the Los Angeles area. He has won and placed in many urban fixed gear events around the Los Angeles area as well as a few events around the nation and continues the brakeless way. He says it's how he found it and thats the way he'll keep it, and goes on to say "A track bike with a brake?, If you never take off you're brake you'll never learn to skid to save you're life"

Instagram: @alonsotal
Facebook: www.facebook.com/alonsotal

Dante Young

Probably one of the youngest riders on the team, from Burbank California Dante has proven time and time again that aged does not matter, but sheer power does. He started to ride bikes purely for the fitness, but once he found out about racing, well that changed everything for him. Not only does he compete in the fixed gear Criterium’s, Dante also races road USAC, as well as on the track. Keep an eye out for him during the 2015 season!

Instagram: @spinningspokes
Facebook: www.facebook.com/dante.young.754

Alvin Escajeda

began riding track bikes around the same time most of my friends were getting their licenses and cars as a way to get around, and I still do to this day. A friend introduced me to "street races" in 2012 and I was immediately hooked after my first one. Racing alley cats and messenger criteriums in Downtown LA improved my skills a rider and allowed me to compete in events like Red Hook Crit, NACCC, and Fijate as well as USAC Criteriums and Track races. The idea of traveling to new cities to ride and race bikes with friends keeps me pedaling.

Instagram: @alvinescajeda
Facebook: www.facebook.com/alvin.escajeda

Angel Munoz

First started riding fixed gear bikes back in 2012 where the "fixed gear" trend was at its peak in Los Angeles. Back then he only rode a fixed gear bike as a hobby and to socialize with other riders in big group rides. But as he started trying out racing underground fixed gear races that were popping up all over the LA area, he grew to love racing competitively with others. Now he races USAC sanctioned road racing , criteriums,velodrome and, big fixed gear criteriums like Red Hook for Leader x Undefeated.

Instagram: @amunoz_
Facebook: www.facebook.com/angel.munoz.14

Cameron Bond

Started racing BMX at the age of 7 and was instantly hooked; racing most of my childhood. He was eventually introduced to road cycling at 14 when his uncle invited him on trip with his boy scout troop, riding from San Francisco to Los Angeles. It wasn't until later when he sold his car and started to commute to work that he fell in love with the cyclist lifestyle. The cycling scene in Los Angeles was so big and vibrant, he was able to meet people who loved to ride their bike everyday as much as I. The racing aspect of bikes was reignited by the 2013 Wolfpack Hustle Marathon Crash Race and from there he knew if he worked hard and trained hard, he could become an accomplished cyclist.

Instagram: @_cameronbond
Facebook: www.facebook.com/withdrawnfromreality

Ronnie Toth

If there is an endurance race, Ronnie Toth has done it. He grew up running marathons which then led to 4 full Ironman Triathlons, where he then shifted into 12 and 24 hour mountain bike racing and then USAC categorized road cycling, where he went from a Cat 4 to a Cat 1 in three months. He landed in a coma after a crash at the NCC 2014 MBGP and made a full recovery in record time where he now races Red Hook and extensive track bike events under the Leader X Undefeated race team banner.

Instagram: @thriveontoth
Facebook: www.facebook.com/TothTothin
